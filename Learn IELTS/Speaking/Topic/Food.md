### **Practice Set 1**

#### **Question 1 of 2**

**"What's your favorite food?"**

This is a very common question to start the topic. The goal is to name a specific dish and use descriptive adjectives to explain why you like it.

---

#### **1. Vocabulary Bank (Keyword Bank)**

| Simple Idea | Upgraded Vocabulary / Collocations (Band 6.5+) |
| :--- | :--- |
| My favorite food is... | My **all-time favorite** is...; My **go-to dish** is...; I'm a **huge fan of**... |
| It tastes good | It's incredibly **flavorful**; It's so **delicious/mouth-watering**; It has a **rich and savory** taste |
| About the dish | The **broth is aromatic**; A perfect **combination of** sweet, sour, and spicy; It's a very **traditional dish** |
| Why I like it | It's my ultimate **comfort food** (food that makes you feel good); It reminds me of my childhood |

---

#### **2. Sentence Structures (How to Build Your Answer)**

A great structure is **"Name the Dish + Describe its Taste/Qualities."**

*   **Structure A:** "Without a doubt, my **all-time favorite** is... I particularly love the..."
*   **Structure B:** "That's a tough choice, but I'd have to say I'm a **huge fan of**... The reason I like it so much is because of its **rich and savory** flavor."
*   **Structure C:** "My **go-to dish** is definitely... For me, nothing beats the perfect **combination of**..."

---

#### **3. Full Answer Example (Band 7.0)**

> "**Without a doubt, my go-to dish is Vietnamese Pho.** I'm a huge fan of the **rich and aromatic broth**, and the perfect **combination of** the tender beef, rice noodles, and fresh herbs is just incredible. For me, it's the ultimate **comfort food**."

**Analysis of the example:**
*   It gives a strong, direct answer.
*   It uses excellent, specific food vocabulary ("go-to dish," "rich and aromatic broth," "combination of," "comfort food").
*   It clearly explains *why* the speaker likes the food by describing its key components.
*   The length is perfect for a Part 1 answer.
