Your choice of every **lexical item** must be **precise**, as **lexical ambiguity** can weaken your claims. **Assuming that** your research is sound, you should be able to **validate a claim** and **prove an assertion**.

It's a **straightforward process** in theory, but difficult in practice. You must **consistently** support your main point. **Paradoxically**, a more **concise** and focused essay is often more powerful than a longer, rambling one. The goal is to present your ideas with such clarity that the reader is left with no **doubt**. You must be prepared to **argue a point** and **defend an assertion** during your final review, which is often more intense than the initial process to **submit a thesis**.
